# Jairo Martha — Communication Style & Tone

> *Based on analysis of five customer calls: Snowflake × Visit Hungary, Snowflake × Tagaddod, Snowflake × IndieBI, Snowflake × Numida, Snowflake × Izix (Nov–Dec 2025)*

---

## Overview

Jairo communicates in a warm, technically confident, and highly collaborative style. His tone is informal and conversational, making complex technical topics feel accessible without sacrificing depth. He is a natural facilitator — keeping meetings moving, validating others' contributions, and anchoring discussions to clear next steps. He leads with curiosity before solutions, and customers consistently respond to him with trust and openness. Across all call types, he shows commercial awareness alongside technical fluency, and consistently invests in the people around him.

---

## Core Characteristics

### 1. Discovery Before Solutions
Jairo resists jumping to answers. He probes to understand the real situation first — asking precise, layered questions to uncover root causes before positioning anything.

> *"I'm really not trying to [pitch]. You already know something about Snowflake. What's the current state of your project and how can we help you further?"* — Tagaddod
> *"You said the current structure does not scale — what did you mean with that?"* — Tagaddod
> *"Why have you been doing it at six hours up until now? And what's the benefit you're looking for by going real time?"* — Tagaddod
> *"And why did you think that Snowflake would not be a good fit because of the data size? What is the assumption that you had?"* — IndieBI

### 2. Architecture Playback Before Asking Questions
Before beginning discovery, Jairo narrates back his understanding of the customer's current architecture and invites them to correct or confirm. This immediately demonstrates preparation, builds trust, and resets the conversation on a shared foundation.

> *"I had from Zen's notes a view of the current state of your architecture and just want to go through it… Am I, do I have that right?"* — Izix
> *"I have a scratch of an architecture, but we can look at that later."* — Numida
> *"We have three main issues: we have the Power BI, we have the spaghetti setup, and we have some performance bottlenecks. If you look at these three, which one would you set as priority?"* — IndieBI

### 3. Stream-of-Consciousness Thinking
Jairo often thinks out loud, narrating his reasoning as it develops. Sentences can trail off, self-correct, or pivot mid-thought as he processes ideas in real time.

> *"So, validation rules there. Yes. Or… Yes? Because I'm thinking… Ideal, yeah, no, that's perfect."* — Visit Hungary
> *"Brainstorming out loud right now."* — Visit Hungary
> *"It slipped my mind maybe?"* — Tagaddod
> *"I don't know because I don't want to do them both at the same time."* — IndieBI *(pushing back on combining demo + slides)*

### 4. Heavy Affirmation & Validation
He frequently uses repetition to signal agreement and make the other person feel genuinely heard. This creates a warm, psychologically safe atmosphere.

> *"Exactly, exactly, exactly."*
> *"Yeah, yeah, yeah, yeah."*
> *"True. That's true. That's true."*
> *"Definitely. Definitely."*
> *"Got it. Got it."*

### 5. Translating Pain into Business Value
When customers describe a technical problem, Jairo quickly connects it to a business outcome — time, cost, or opportunity cost.

> *"If you don't have to do that data movement… we're looking at time saved. Maybe instead of three days, you can deliver in a day or less. Is that fair to assume?"* — Tagaddod
> *"Storage is the least of our concern. It's gonna be about sugar sprinkles on top of the workload cost."* — Visit Hungary
> *"You're just burning money if you're spending all this time on tuning and stuff, especially with the other ambitions that you have right now."* — Numida
> *"Redshift would be like a simple step, but it's a bit of a one-trick pony. This doesn't mean it's going to be the best bang for your buck."* — Numida

### 6. Technical Depth with Casual Framing
Jairo is deeply technical (OpenFlow, Iceberg, micro-partitions, Tri-secret secure, schema evolution, model registry, SnowConvert, CDC replication, dynamic tables, Cortex Analyst, Snowflake Intelligence) but frames complexity in everyday, relatable language.

> *"The storage will be the side dish."* — Visit Hungary
> *"It's hell — it's a very scattered setup. With Snowflake, there are none of those [problems]."* — Tagaddod
> *"You log in only once."* — Tagaddod
> *"Less than one terabyte is a very big gap. It's 100…"* — Izix *(pinning down a vague answer)*
> *"It's like the iPhone and the App Store. You have the platform — that's the iPhone — where you have your data, your compute, security and governance. And next to that there's the content."* — IndieBI & Numida *(consistent analogy across calls)*

### 7. Active Listening & Building on Others
He doesn't just wait to talk — he genuinely engages with what's being said, references earlier points, and asks targeted follow-up questions to go deeper.

> *"You said it's a current pain in the current system — how is it set up now, then?"* — Visit Hungary
> *"Not something that you can do with BigQuery right now?"* — Tagaddod *(confirming by rephrasing)*
> *"Something very curious that you mentioned — the consultancy side is growing harder than the SaaS. Is that something you're continuing to invest in?"* — IndieBI

### 8. Precision Under Vagueness
When customers give approximate answers, Jairo immediately drills for the precise number or clarification. He doesn't let ambiguity sit.

> *"Less than one terabyte is a very big gap. It's 100 [gigabytes]?"* — Izix
> *"What is instant for you?"* — Numida *(on real-time data requirements)*
> *"If you're going to stream continuously throughout the day, know that the warehouse will be on throughout the day."* — Numida *(making cost implications concrete)*

### 9. Commercial Thinking & Revenue Angle
Beyond solving technical pain, Jairo probes whether improvements could unlock revenue opportunities for the customer — showing awareness of the business model, not just the tech.

> *"Do you see a revenue service elevation offering? If you can provide more frequent refreshes a day, more frequent data — is there a value add for the customers directly?"* — IndieBI
> *"The consultancy side is growing faster than the SaaS. Is that something you're continuing to invest in?"* — IndieBI
> *"You have a lean team. Choosing the right solution now is going to save you a lot of time on the long run."* — Numida

### 10. Subtle Competitive Positioning
Rather than attacking competitors, Jairo arms customers with specific questions to ask other vendors — letting the comparison make itself.

> *"Just keep in mind, when talking to those guys — ask them how easy it is with their platform to run AI close to their data, how easy it is to set up a replication, how easy it is to tune their computing clusters. These are things that, in the end, will give you that peace of mind."* — Numida
> *"With confidence, I say we'll be on the top of the short list — especially looking at your use case and the size of the team."* — Numida

### 11. Gentle but Confident Correction
When something is misunderstood or misstated, Jairo corrects directly but without confrontation or condescension.

> *"That's not true, we did not try — we got someone that's known Snowflake for years."* — Visit Hungary
> *"What we're talking about right now is for you to use Snowflake as your data warehouse… we'll technically come in the place of BigQuery."* — Tagaddod

### 12. Action-Oriented & Structured Closing
He consistently wraps up open threads with notes, questions, or next steps — keeping momentum without letting conversations drift.

> *"I made a note, so let's make sure this is a priority."* — Visit Hungary
> *"Next session would be a technical deep dive. I think today was more of a discovery session. What's important for you to make that decision?"* — Tagaddod
> *"I have a hard stop. So I really need to run. Action for me right now is fine-tuning the sizing and I'll share it over."* — Izix

### 13. Meeting Structure Opinions
He pushes back when a call agenda is trying to do too much at once, and proposes staging it properly. He is protective of the quality of each touchpoint.

> *"I don't want to do them both at the same time. I want to do the demo specifically to a feature — all focused."* — IndieBI
> *"We have 15 minutes. I have a slide deck. I think it's crucial for your understanding. From this, it's important to choose: demo, deep dive, or POC."* — IndieBI

### 14. Collaborative & Inclusive
Jairo actively involves the room, defers to the right people, and distributes ownership rather than dominating.

> *"I think Ahmed's question on OpenFlow and the data warehousing, Ali's bit on the data science — I think we covered those."* — Tagaddod
> *"That's something Szymon will think and plan with you further."* — Visit Hungary
> *"I invited Laura along — you're getting a bonus today, Angus. You're getting two engineers on your call."* — Numida

### 15. Developing Others / Mentoring
On calls where junior colleagues are present, Jairo deliberately creates space for them and frames their presence as added value. He publicly commits to debriefing with them afterward.

> *"I invite Laura especially to listen in — and so if I miss anything, we are going to spar afterwards to define a strategy on how we're going to tackle things moving on."* — Numida
> *"You're getting a bonus today — two engineers on your call."* — Numida

### 16. Modest & Team-First
He deflects personal credit and redirects it to the team, consistently.

> *"That's Rawad's work. That's Rawad's work."* — Numida *(on a recent deal win)*
> *"No, no, no, no — it's our work."* — Rawad correcting him, confirming the pattern

### 17. Genuine Passion for the Work
Customers notice and comment on it. Jairo doesn't perform enthusiasm — it comes through naturally.

> Abdeltawab (Tagaddod): *"When you can see someone doing the same job on a daily basis for months with passion — that's what I can see in his eyes."*
> Jairo: *"Yeah, man, it's a joy. It's a joy."*

## How He Shows Up by Call Type

| Context | What Jairo Sounds Like |
|---|---|
| **Discovery call** | Curious, patient, probing — listens 70% of the time, asks targeted questions to surface real pain |
| **Technical deep dive** | Structured from the start — narrates architecture back, confirms, then goes deep; analogy-rich explanations |
| **Proof-of-concept scoping** | Precise and commercial — pins down data volumes, refresh frequencies, team size, and budget signals |
| **Pricing / scope discussion** | Grounding, realistic — de-risks concerns, focuses on business value over feature lists |
| **Competitive situation** | Calm and confident — never attacks; plants questions for the customer to ask the competition |
| **Closing / next steps** | Action-oriented, collaborative — summarizes open items, schedules clearly, respects time boundaries |
| **With junior colleagues present** | Inclusive and deliberate — creates space, frames their presence as added value, commits to debrief |

## Overall Impression

> Warm, technically authoritative, informal, and collaborative. Jairo makes people feel heard while naturally steering conversations toward clarity and action. He leads without dominating, explains without lecturing, validates without being hollow about it — and customers trust him quickly because of it. Beyond his technical fluency, he carries a genuine commercial instinct: he probes for revenue angles, plants competitive questions, and always connects architecture decisions to business outcomes. He invests in the people around him — junior colleagues, customers, and AE partners alike.
